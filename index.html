Tiger Woods A Generation of Dominance

A timeline of the life of Tiger Woods:

  • December 1975 - Born Eldrick T. Woods to Earl Woods, who gave him the nickname "Tiger " after a friend with the same nickname.
  • June 1976 - Sees his father hitting balls into a net, begins imitating his swing.
  • October 1978 - Appears on the Mike Douglas Show, hitting drives and putting against comedian Bob Hope.
  • 1979 - Shoots a 48 on nine holes at the Navy Golf Course at the age of 3.
  • May 1982 - Makes his first hole-in-one at the age of six.
  • July 1984 - Wins first of six Junior World titles, winning the 9-10 age division at 8 years old.
  • 1991 - Wins first U.S. Junior, the first of his record six consecutive USGA titles.
  • February 1992 - Competes in first PGA TOUR event as a 16-year-old high school sophomore, missing the cut by six strokes.
  • 1994 - Wins his first of three consecutive U.S. Amateur Championships.
  • 1995 - Wins NCAA Championship as a student at Stanford.
  • August 1996 - Wins final U.S. Amateur despite being down 5 strokes with less than 18 holes remaining. Three days later, he announces he is turning pro.
  • October 1996 - Wins first PGA TOUR event at the Las Vegas Invitational.
  • January 1997 - Wins his third PGA TOUR event in only 9 starts as a pro, closing out Tom Lehman in a sudden-death playoff.
  • April 1997 - Dominates 1997 Masters with record-breaking 18-under par en route to a 12-stroke victory in his first major championship appearance as a pro.
  • August 1999 - Wins his second major at the 1999 PGA Championship at Medinah Country Club.
  • June 2000 - Breaks the record for the largest winning margin in major championship history by winning the 2000 U.S. Open by 15 strokes at Pebble Beach.
  • July 2000 - Completes career Grand Slam by winning the 2000 Open Championship by eight shots at St. Andrews.
  • August 2000 - Wins third straight major at the 2000 PGA Championship in a three-hole playoff at Valhalla.
  • March 2001 - Wins first PLAYERS championship, often considered the "fifth major, " at the classic TPC Sawgrass.
  • April 2001 - Wins the 2001 Masters, completing the "Tiger Slam, " giving him four consecutive major wins dating back to the previous year.
  • March 2002 - Wins Bay Hill, his third consecutive victory in the event and his 30th PGA TOUR title.
  • April 2002 - Closes out Retief Goosen at Augusta to win the 2002 Masters, his second consecutive victory there.
  • June 2002 - Bests Phil Micklelson by three shots to win the 2002 U.S. Open at Bethpage Black, completing the first half of the Grand Slam.
  • August 2004 - Passes Greg Norman for the most weeks at No. 1 in the world for a total of 332 weeks (over 6 years).< /li>
  • April 2005 - Wins 2005 Masters, joining Arnold Palmer and Jack Nicklaus as the only players to win the event four or more times.
  • July 2005 - Completes wire-to-wire victory at St. Andrews to claim the 2005 Open Championship over Colin Montgomer ie.
  • July 2006 - Holds off Ernie Els, Jim Furyk and Sergio Garcia to win the 2006 Open Championship at Royal Liverpool by two shots, successfully defending his title from the previous year.
  • August 2006 - Wins the 2006 Buick Open to mark his 50th career PGA TOUR victory at just 30 years old.
  • August 2006 - On the third longest course in major championship history, maintains 5-shot lead to capture the 2006 PGA Championship at Medinah Country Club.
  • January 2007 - In one of the most dominant stretches in the history of golf, wins his seventh consecutive event, taking home the title at the 2007 Buick Invitational. Only Byron Nelson has won more events in a row (11), and he did so in 1945.
  • August 2007 - Wins the 2007 PGA Championship at Southern Hills, despite an average temperature of 100 degrees over the course of the tournament.
  • June 2008 - Defeats Rocco Mediate in memorable 19-hole playoff to win the 2008 U.S. Open at Torrey Pines despite having a torn ACL and two stress fractures. His 15-foot, downhill birdie putt on the 72nd hole to force a playoff remains one of the most memorable moments in his career (pictured above).
  • August 2009 - Wins the 2009 Bridgestone Invitational at Firestone to clinch his 70th career PGA TOUR title, joining Jack Nicklaus (73) and Sam Snead (82) as the only other PGA TOUR players win 70 or more events.
  • October 2010 - Breaks own record for consecutive weeks at No. 1; his 281-week reign lasted from June 2005 to October 2010.
  • June 2012 - Ties Jack Nicklaus with 73 career wins after coming from four shots back to win the 2012 Memorial tournament at Muirfield Village. In the final round, his hole-out on 16 propelled him to victory.
  • May 2013 - Wins his second PLAYERS Championship, finishing two shots ahead of three runners-up at TPC Sawgrass.
